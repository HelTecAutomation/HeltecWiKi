:::tip
As previously introduced in the LoRaWAN overview, we offer a wide range of LoRa/LoRaWAN node devices, which are mainly categorized into three types: Maker/Dev Kit, Module, and Plug & Play.
:::

This section focuses on Plug & Play devices, which are designed with a ready-to-use concept, featuring preconfigured parameters, remote management support, and strong platform compatibility, enabling rapid deployment.


**The following are mainstream Plug & Play  devices.**

| LoRa Node           | Descrption    |
|-----------------|---------------|
|[HRI 4851 LoRaWAN Wireless Converter](https://heltec.org/project/rs485-lorawan-wireless-converter/) | A device that converts 485 bus into LoRa, LTE, and Ethernet communication. |
|[HRI 4851 LoRa Wireless Converter](https://heltec.org/project/rs485-lora-wireless-converter/) | HRI-4851 converts 485 bus into LoRa. |
|[HRI 4852 LoRa Wireless Converter](https://heltec.org/project/rs485-lora-wireless-converter/)| HRI-4852 connect HRI-4851 to server via ETH or LTE.|
|[HRI 3631 Wireless Aggregator Sensor Docker](https://heltec.org/project/hri-3631/)|A solar-powered, ultra-low-power LoRa/LoRaWAN outdoor aggregator with long-range connectivity and Fast Link support. |
|[HRI 3632 Wireless Aggregator Bus Transformer](https://heltec.org/project/hri-3632/)| a low-power, long-range outdoor LoRaWAN sensor converter with GPIO support and IP66 protection.|
|[HRI 3633 Wireless Aggregator Valve Controller](https://heltec.org/project/hri-3633/)| A solar-powered LoRa/LoRaWAN valve controller for outdoor pulse valve and meter control.|
